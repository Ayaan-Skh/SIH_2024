1. The Preamble

The Preamble serves as the Constitution's introduction, outlining the guiding principles of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity. It reflects the core values and the vision for a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.

2. Directive Principles of State Policy (Part IV)

Directive Principles provide a framework for the state to follow in governance, aiming to create a just society. Though non-justiciable, they guide the government in policy-making to promote social and economic welfare.

3. Fundamental Rights (Part III)

Fundamental Rights are the essential rights guaranteed to all citizens, ensuring equality, freedom, protection against discrimination, and the right to life and personal liberty. They serve as the cornerstone of democracy, protecting individuals from state infringement.

4. Fundamental Duties (Part IV A)

Fundamental Duties outline the moral obligations of all citizens to help promote a spirit of patriotism and to uphold the unity and integrity of the nation. These duties encourage responsible citizenship and reinforce the values of the Constitution.
